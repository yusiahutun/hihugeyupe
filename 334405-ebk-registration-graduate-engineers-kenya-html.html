EBK registration for graduate engineers in Kenya

Publish date: 2024-04-13

Engineering is ranked among the most respectable careers globally. It is also a popular career choice among the youth in Kenya. For you to become an engineer, you should undertake an engineering course in a certified institution of higher learning. After completion of your studies, you will need to be registered with an engineering body. This allows you to be recognized as an engineer and allowed to undertake duties in the field. Every engineering graduate in Kenya is required to undertake EBK registration.

The abbreviations "EBK" stand for the Engineers Board of Kenya. This body was created through the Engineers Act 2011 and awarded the mandate to oversee and regulate the engineering career in Kenya. Every engineer, whether a Kenyan citizen or foreigner is required by law to be registered with the Engineers Board of Kenya. After receiving the board’s approval, the engineer is then given permission to practice his or her trade in the country.

Some of the core functions of this body include:

  • Receiving, considering, and making decisions on all registration enquiries for graduate engineers in Kenya
  • The storage and preservation of the register of approved engineers
  • To carry out enquiries related to the application and registering of engineers
  • Assessing, approving or declining the approval of foreign engineers who would like to deploy their skillset in the country
  • Aid in the preparation of curriculum that is needed for registration and administering of professional exams needed in the registration process.

EBK registration requirements

For a graduate engineer to qualify for registration, they should possess the following documents:

  • A copy of your degree certificate and any other relevant academic certificates
  • A copy of the National Identity Card (ID)
  • A photocopy of your original Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) certificate
  • 2 passport photos (coloured)

These documents should be certified by the Commissioner of Oaths before commencing the EBK registration.

For the foreign students who would wish to register with EBK, the following documents and details are required according to the institution:

  • Certified copies of their academic transcripts
  • A contact person from the university where they undertook the engineering course
  • Email and physical address of the university where they undertook the course

EBK registration process

There is a specific EBK registration procedure that an applicant should follow for successful registration. The graduate engineer registration process can be completed on an application form collected from the EBK offices or online.

The following is the registration process that a graduate engineer can undertake at the EBK offices.

  • Fill an application form that can be picked at the EBK’s Customer Information Desk
  • Attach all the copies of the required documents which are the academic documents, identity card, and the two passport photos.
  • Deposit Ksh 4,000 application fee at the EBK’s bank account to obtain a bank slip that will be attached to the application form. The board strictly does not accept cash.
  • After filling the application form, embed the documents and the bank slip, afterward leave the application documents at the customer care and information desk.
  • You will then receive an acknowledgement letter that indicates that you have initiated the graduate engineer registration process and that the application is under review by the board.
  • You will be notified through a letter on the board’s decision pertaining to your application on whether it has been accepted or rejected.
  • It is important to note that the board reserves the right to accept or reject the application.
  • If your application is considered okay, you will get with a registration certificate.

The Engineering Board of Kenya online registration

Other than physically heading to their offices, it is also possible to do EBK registration online. These EBK registration forms can be found on the EBK website.

Follow this process to complete the EBK graduate registration online:

  • Click on the Engineers portal link. In the personal details section, you will be required to fill your full names, country, passport number for foreigners, ID number for the Kenyan citizens, telephone number and email address.
  • You are required to create a password for your account and then click update. You will get an alert to check your email to continue the registration process.
  • A URL will be sent to your email. Click on it to sign in to your new account using an ID number and password.
  • Enter the necessary personal details as well as upload a professional photo. You can then go ahead with registration by selecting "apply for registration" button.
  • Prior to finalising your registration, it is an obligation to read and accept the Engineers Act 2011, the privacy policy and terms and conditions. The Act guides the EBK board activities and also provides guidelines for its members’ conduct and ethics.
  • Scan and upload the certified documentation. The documents required should be genuine and a relevant degree from an accredited institution is part of the required paperwork. Your postal address, in addition to official names of the commissioner of oaths, should also be fully displayed on the stamp.
  • Download and print the invoice against which you will make the payment. You will receive the receipt after 24 hours from the time of payment. Then download the receipt as it will be needed for submission.
  • Click to download the form and attach the invoice and receipt then deliver to the Engineers Board of Kenya offices or send them through the portal.

The board seeks to foster an accountability framework by approving qualified engineers with high levels of work ethic and excellence in the country.
