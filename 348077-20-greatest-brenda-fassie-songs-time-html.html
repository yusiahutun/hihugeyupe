20 greatest Brenda Fassie songs of all time

Publish date: 2024-02-09

Brenda Nokuzola Fassie was born in Langa, Cape Town, South Africa. The musical star was named after Brenda Lee, an American artist who sung and performed pop-rock and country music. She never got to grow up with her father as he died when the singer was two years old. Brenda started making money off music while still a teen. With the help of her pianist mother, the Cape Town native started singing for tourists and made some cash for herself. Brenda Fassie songs still sound good today as they did decades ago.

Before going as a solo artist, Brenda had been part of a group in Johannesburg called Joy and a recording group that went by the name Brenda and the Big Dudes. She enjoyed her time on the limelight as she was called to perform in South Africa's capital and across the country.

Her performances attracted big crowds and earned her millions of fans in her home country and the rest of the world. Some of the songs that made her gain popularity include Weekend Special, Nakupenda and Too Late for Mama.

Greatest Brenda Fassie songs

The following are 20 greatest Brenda Fassie songs of all time.

1. Nomakanjani

Nomakanjani is a love song where the singer swears to stick by her lover no matter what. Brenda's vocals alternate with a male voice throughout the song as each of them profess their love and commitment for each other. Nomakanjani is an exceptional song to play for naysayers who disapprove of others' relationships because they feel one party deserves better.

Like every song dedicated to mothers, this Brenda Fassie tune did not disappoint. A remorseful Brenda starts by apologizing to her mother for the wrongs she has done. She continues the first verse by saying that she will change as she has learned her lesson. The lovely South African sound can be felt during the interlude. The track may sound like a regret tune, but the singer's smooth voice makes you forget the sombre theme.
