Top 10 female rhumba artists of all time: Who ranks at the top?

Publish date: 2024-03-09

Rhumba music is loved across Africa and the world. Ask any rhumba lover who their favourite artists are, and the chances are that they will mention the likes of Papa Wemba, Kanda Bongo Man, Awilo Longomba, and Mose Se Sengo. Not many recognize female rhumba artists as the genre is mainly men. The musicians you will hear about are often recognized as backup singers or dancers to the big names. Despite the men being the majority, a few women also tried and had successful careers and male legends.

To make it big as a rhumba musician, you need to be flexible, versatile and have a singing style that will appeal to the masses. Many of the established musicians, whether male or females, understand that rhumba is bigger than Congo, despite it being the original country.

That is why you will find both male and female Congolese singers infuse foreign elements in some of their songs to charm their international audience. They do this smoothly without losing their original style. The outfits they rock when performing is another spectacular feature.

Top 10 female rhumba artists

Africa’s most desired female singers are known to be bold and fierce. They have filled the world’s biggest concert halls, and people have long loved, cried, lived, and danced to the rhythms of their beats. Here is a list of the top 10 best female rhumba artists of all time.

1. Abeti Masikini

Elisabeth Finant, popularly known as Abeti Masikini was born in 1954 and grew among the most notable names in the Belgian Congo musical scene. Abeti was soulful energetic and dedicated her life to music. She enjoyed making music in soukous, Congolese rhumba, folk music, and even blues. Some of her songs that still get played include:

  • Bebe Matoko
  • We Muloko Wangu
  • Kilki Bamba
  • Viens Mo Amour
  • Bi Suivra Suivra

Abeti’s life was cut short on 9 November 1954 when she succumbed to cancer.

2. M'bilia Bel

M'bilia Bel is perhaps the most distinguished female rhumba musician to come out of the Congo. She is not called the Queen of Congolese and African rhumba for nothing. M'bilia sings her heart out and is hailed both in Central Africa and East Africa as the best female rhumba musician ever to grace the scene.

M'bilia Bel started performing professionally in the 1980s. She was first a backup singer for Abeti Masikini and Sam Mangwana before going solo. Some of M'bilia Bel classic songs include:

  • Mobaly Na Ngai Wana,
  • Sale Ou Sucre
  • Nakei Nairobi
  • La Beaute D’une Femme
  • Ba Gerants Ya Mabala

3. Tshala Muana

Tshala Muana first considered music a professional career when she joined the Congolese band Tsheke Tsheke Love as a dancer in 1971. The 63-year-old rhumba, Afrobeat, and Lingala artiste found her foot after singing with the ban and went on to release singles that were played in her country and beyond.

Tshala Muana has more than a dozen studio albums and collaboration albums to her name. The Soukos and rhumba queen is known for songs like:

  • Lekela Muadi
  • Karibou Yangu
  • Nasi Nabali
  • Malu Deux
  • Kalume

Tshala Muana still performs, though not as much as her hay days due to her stroke.

4. M'Pongo Love

M'Pongo Love started singing in the church choir when young. Born in Boma, D.R.C in 1956, she contracted Polio when young, which had her use prosthesis for a while. However, her love for music could be seen as she enjoyed following her father to the church choir.

M'Pongo Love left school at 19 to pursue music, which she did not regret as music paid off. Her unique slightly nasal voice and precise intonations in her singing style are what made fans love her. Popular M'Pongo Love's songs are:

  • Fetiche M’Pongo
  • Mona aElima
  • Bakake
  • Bukueri Ya Ngufu

M'Pongo Love passed on in January 1990.

5. Abby Surya

Abby Surya was born in Kinshasa. She sang while young and officially started her professional musical journey in 1984. Abby closely sang and performed with Abeti Masikini, the renowned musician. She learned most of her skills as a musician in Abeti’s band called The Redoubtable.

Abby sang with The Redoubtable for a while and soon left the band to pursue a solo career. While a solo artiste, many established Congolese musicians noticed her skills and asked to collaborate with her. Abby has worked with prominent names like Maika Munan, Koffi Olomide, Arlito Lasa, Suzy Kaseya, Aurlus Mabele, and Papam Wemba, among others.

6. Alicios Theluji

Alicios Theluji was born in Goma, D.R.C., but spent most of her years in Kenya. The Congolese born rhumba and Lingala star first knew she could sing in the early 2000s. The 35-year-old artiste recorded her first song in 2011 and has enjoyed a successful music career ever since. Famous songs by Alicious include:

  • Posa Ya Bolingo
  • Loved by You
  • Anita
  • Fundi

The themes in Alicios Theluji often revolve around societal happenings.

7. Christy Lova

Christy Lova is one of the many popular female rhumba singers. Born Christelle Ntesa Love, Christy has found a way of appealing to the new generation. The Congolese musician has had an easy start in the industry, being the daughter of the late musician Ntesa Dalienst, and keeping his legacy alive through her music.

Many of her fans appreciated her more when she released Bina Nanga Na Respect, a rendition of his late father's greatest record. The singer's other songs you will enjoy include:

  • To Bina
  • Simba Nga
  • Mam Lova
  • Mytho

8. Cindy Le Coeur

Born on 11 November 1982 in the Democratic Republic of Congo, Cindy Le Coeur has progressed to be one of the most sought after female Congolese musicians in her prime. The dancer, singer and songwriter has worked with notable names in Congo.

She has collaborated with Koffi Olomide, among many. Cindy was a member of Koffi’s Quartier Latin International band. She is adored by rhumba and Soukou lovers. Top Cindy Le Coeur's songs are:

  • Lili Kaniki
  • Eputsha
  • Fouta Djalon
  • L’eau-l’eau-l’eau

9. Yondo Sister

You will never feel low when playing Yondo Sister’s music. The Congolese musician born Yondo Kusala Denise in Zaire in 1958 established herself as the finest when she came to the industry. Yondo Sister is hailed as The Queen of Soukous by her Congolese fans, a title she deserves because of how great she is as a performer.

She is great in rhumba, and Kwasa Kwasa and continues to give her fans the ultimate experience when performing in concerts. Yondo sister has six albums and has released many hit songs. They include:

  • Bazo
  • Mbuta Mutu
  • Mwana Kento
  • Deviation
  • Tcha Tcha.

10. L'Or Mbongo

Born L'Or Lemba-Mbongo, the 43-year Congolese musician, is ranked among the most celebrated female singers from Congo. Coming from a tough childhood helped L'Or Mbongo write better.
