Who is Bob Newhart?

Newhart is an actor and comedian who achieved fame as a stand-up performer and later starred in television sitcoms. He was born on September 5, 1929, in Oak Park, Illinois, the United States of America, and will be turning 93 years old in 2022.

Newhart's parents were Julia Pauline (1900-1994), a housewife and George David Newhart (1900-1985), a part-owner of a plumbing and heating supply company.

Bob’s parents were both of Irish descent, with his father having some German ancestry. Therefore, this means that Bob Newhart is of mixed ethnicity.

When Bob was young, he attended Roman Catholic schools in the Chicago region. The schools include St. Catherine of Siena Grammar School in Oak Park and St. Ignatius College Prep (high school).

He graduated from high school in 1947 and went to college at Loyola University of Chicago. Here, the actor graduated with a bachelor's degree in Business Management. He was immediately drafted into the United States Army and was part of the unit that took part in the Korean war as a personnel manager.

Bob Newhart’s career

You have likely seen quite a handful of Bob Newhart's movies and TV shows, but how did it all start? After abandoning his law degree and leaving school in 1956, Bob started working a string of jobs. He eventually got a job as a bookkeeper and took acting roles in a local theatrical stock company on the side.

At the time, he used to engage in humorous telephone conversations with his friend, whereby he improvised characters. Newhart and his friend proceeded to record these funny conversations and tried to sell them to radio stations.

The business endeavour was not as successful as Newhart and his friend expected, but it was fruitful as he captured the attention of a local disc jockey. The disc jockey gave Rob Newhart a chance to perform comedic monologues on the radio. This opportunity allowed the comedian to get an even more significant role and a recording contract with Warner Brothers in 1959.
