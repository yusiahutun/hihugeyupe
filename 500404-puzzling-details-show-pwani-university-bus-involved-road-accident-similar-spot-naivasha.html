Another Pwani University Bus Crashed Near Naivasha in 2015 after Brakes Failed, Details Emerge

Publish date: 2024-10-02
  • Pwani University experienced a similar accident as the one which occurred in Naivasha on Thursday, March 30
  • A former student of the campus said that he was among the students who were involved in a similar road accident in 2015 in the same Naivasha spot
  • He narrated to TUKO.co.ke how their accident also happened due to brake failure and they resorted to prayers

A former student of Pwani University has revealed that another school bus from the institution also got into a similar accident to the one which happened earlier today.

On Thursday, March 30, the university's bus crashed into a matatu in Naivasha after it reportedly lost its brakes as it was ferrying students to KUSO games in Eldoret.

Similar accident in 2015

Speaking to TUKO.co.ke, a former student of the campus said that he was among the students who were involved in a similar road accident in 2015, which also happened as he and his fellow students were being ferried for games in Bondo.

Omollo Charles said the bus lost its brakes in the same area in Naivasha where today's accident happened.

"We had made it up to that area when the driver called Kahindi lost his brakes. We lost the brakes, and the driver notified Ken and me that he could not brake. By then, I was the Deputy Organising Secretary of the Union, and so sports were fully under me," Omollo told this writer."We swerved for some time, missing oncoming vehicles, including a semi-trailer, until we hit the Kawira Bus Sacco from Behind. He is the one that saved our lives," he added.

Omollo said the bus from Kawira Sacco saved their lives as it did not avoid being hit, and it held onto the Pwani University bus until it stopped on its own, metres to the then Naivasha roadblock.

"That bus driver later told us that he had understood that we had lost brakes and that's why he did not panic, he just got his brakes slowly to help us slow down even after being hit from the rear," Omollo recounted.

Luckily for them, no one lost their lives in the incident, and they survived with varying degrees of injuries.

Sadly, the university's Sports Officer, identified as Ken, survived the 2015 accident but died during the Thursday accident.

"This time, he got unlucky. He was a great man, and so were the comrades who have left us," Omollo added.

Pwani University students mourn driver of ill-fated bus

In other news, heartbroken students from Pwani University mourned their driver who died in the ghastly road accident.

Students from the university could not help but mourn their driver, who was among the casualties.

"Our pilot. Go easy, uncle," a student said after posting his picture.
