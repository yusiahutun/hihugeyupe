The Samaritan setbook summary notes, characters, theme, and guide

Publish date: 2024-03-29

The Samaritan is a newly KICD-approved compulsory play set book. The Samaritan setbook play exposes the greed and desires of political leaders and inspires individuals to shun evil for the good of society. It’s a fantastic read that brings to date all that has gone wrong in African states.

TABLE OF CONTENTS

The Samaritan narrates the story of two secondary students and a teacher in Maracas municipality who created an application called the Samaritan. The app allows Maracas people to share information about corruption in their municipality and ideas that enhance public welfare.

The Samaritan setbook summary

What is The Samaritan setbook about? The story happens in a fictional municipality; Maracas. The Samaritan setbook play is about students Montano and Alvita and their teacher Nicole, who created a mobile application called the Samaritan.

The app allows people to discuss corruption and share information or ideas that enhance public welfare. Even before its official launch, the app had numerous information about misconduct, corruption, and political captures by Maracas's political leaders.

Segrada secondary school invited Mossi, the Maracas municipality mayor, to officially launch the Samaritan application. But surprisingly, the mayor didn’t launch it on claims of possible misinformation, witch hunt, and lies.

Mossi commanded Nicole to stop the application. But it was late because the app was already in the hands of a government agency.

The Samaritan setbook themes

Themes are the central unifying ideas in books. They are the bigger issues that emerge as the characters pursue their goals. Below are the notable themes in The Samaritan setbook.

1. Corruption

The meeting between Mossi, Bembe, and Harvester shows how corrupt the leaders of Maracas were. Bembe has committed a lot of atrocities, such as the violations of human rights and land grabbing. Conversely, Mossi is guilty of municipal capture; he awards tenders to friends and family.

2. Misuse of power

In the play, it's evident that people in power or leadership positions use their offices to get what they want and to influence certain decisions. Harvester, the municipality CEO, abuses public office by drafting a letter to the government to ban the Samaritan application.

Jaden, the judge, and Harvester agree to hold a meeting for the warring parties to call a truce. During the meeting, they agree that Jaden should file cases and make them disappear by adjourning them. This will help kill any legal moves against them.

They plan to tame Nicole by offering her a job as the municipal consultant with oversight powers on all departments, but she refuses.

3. Fear and courage

There's a sense of fear and courage throughout the play, as revealed by the actions and conversations of some of the characters. Alvita, Montano, and Nicole have the courage to develop and launch an app that exposes the dirt of their most powerful leaders.

Montano and Alvita are fearful about the outcome of the Samaritan app on Nicole after she received a day threat from Mayor Mossi. However, Nicole confirms that everything will be alright and that they must be hopeful that the application uncovers the entire scandals in the municipality.

Mayor Mossi and other leaders fear for their fates when the Samaritan app takes a turn for the worse, and news about the rot in the municipality dominates headlines in the daily newspapers.

4. False accusations

In the play, Bembe and Narine approach Nicole to check her office. Nicole is then arrested for failing to stop the Samaritan app and being stubborn. They also frame her for stealing the school generator they had sent thugs to steal. But Nicole is later released by Jayden, the judge, who found her innocent after a protest by those who attended the hearing.

5. Arguments and disagreements

The theme of arguments and disagreement is evident in The Samaritan set book because most of the play's characters have conflicts. Mossi, the mayor, disagrees with Nicole over the Samaritan mobile app. He denies launching it on counts of possible misinformation and asks Nicole to stop it.

Those in leadership at the municipality disagree with Basdeo, the opposition leader, and insist he is behind the leaked documents on the app. They plan to meet with the councilors to push for a vote of no confidence in Basdeo.
