Publish date: 2024-03-26

Which are the safest neighborhoods in Brooklyn? Well, most parts of Brooklyn are safe. Brooklyn has evolved significantly over the last few decades, with several neighbourhoods gentrifying and the crime rate dropping, making it safer than ever.

Despite being the most populated borough in New York City, Brooklyn still has many places to explore and live. Its neighbourhoods are welcoming and have vibrant cultural scenes and histories, making them alluring to many.

Safest neighbourhoods in Brooklyn to live

Over the last few decades, Brooklyn has transformed into the hottest, trendiest borough in New York City. However, because of its large size and population, finding the best part of Brooklyn to live in or visit can be intimidating.

To help, here is a list of the safest neighborhoods in Brooklyn for families. It has been compiled using data from Area Vibes and Metropolis Moving.

1. Kensington

Kensington is located south of Greenwood Cemetery and Prospect Park. It's predominantly a residential area with different housing types, including brick row houses, apartment buildings, and detached one-family Victorians. The neighbourhood has a diverse population.

Kensington’s main commercial streets are Church Avenue, Coney Island Avenue, McDonald Avenue and Ditmas Avenue. It’s the safest area in Brooklyn, with an estimated annual crime count of 302 per 100,000 people.

2. Crown Heights

Crown Heights spans a huge cross-section of central Brooklyn, that’s from the Brooklyn Botanic Garden on one edge to East New York. The neighbourhood, full of Brooklyn newcomers and old-timers alike, is as vibrant, busy and eclectic as it is wide.

Crown Heights offers access to some of Brooklyn's top attractions and museums, including the Brooklyn Botanic Garden and Brooklyn Museum.

The area has a mix of Caribbean immigrants and Hasidic Jews, making it culturally rich and a destination with plenty of tasty food. The neighbourhood has an estimated total annual crime of 950 per 100,000 people.

3. Dyker Heights

Which part of Brooklyn is the best to live in? Dyker Heights is among the safest parts of Brooklyn. It is a residential community predominantly composed of one—and two-family homes.

The neighbourhood is home to the 242-acre Dyker Beach Park, which has a junior golf centre, a championship golf course, courts and fields for handball, tennis, baseball, bocce, and quiet walking paths. Dyker Heights is considered one of the safest neighbourhoods in Brooklyn, with a total annual crime of 1,273 (estimate) per 100,000 people.

4. Borough Park

Borough Park is a neighbourhood in southwestern Brooklyn. It is home to one of the largest Orthodox Jewish communities outside Israel, and its population continues to grow. Borough Park offers its residents the best balance of a suburban feel while maintaining all the essential conveniences of modern city life.

In addition, the neighbourhood’s 13th Ave Commercial District has some of the city's best shops, restaurants, bakeries, and more. It's also considered one of the safest parts of Brooklyn, with a reported total crime of 1,343 (estimate) per 100,000 people.

5. Wingate

Wingate is a residential neighbourhood in the central part of Brooklyn, New York City. It's known for its stately homes, many of which were constructed in the early 20th century.

Wingate is home to Wingate Park, a famous green space that offers recreational facilities such as handball and basketball courts, a playground, and baseball fields.

This quiet and calm neighbourhood offers easy access to local amenities and transportation, making it desirable to live in. Wingate has an estimated total annual crime of 1,488 per 100,000 people.

6. Gravesend

Gravesend is a diverse, family-oriented neighbourhood along the waterfront of Gravesend Bay. Though it's quieter, there are many recreational activities in the area. Its proximity to Coney Island makes it an excellent destination for beach lovers. Lastly, Gravesend is among the safest places in Brooklyn, with an annual crime rate of 1,890 (estimate) per 100,000 people.

7. Park Slope

Park Slope is a neighbourhood in western Brooklyn, New York City, in the area once known as South Brooklyn. It's known for its open-minded community of young professionals and families. The mix of laid-back bars, indie boutiques, casual residents along Fifth Avenue and pockets of Seventh Avenue cater to its diverse population.

Visitors and locals often head to Prospect Park for outdoor picnics, concerts and jogging. Park Slope’s total annual crime is 2,157 (estimate) per 100,000 people.

8. Bay Ridge

Bay Ridge is a diverse, lush, vibrant neighbourhood in Brooklyn, New York. This neighbourhood is known for its breathtaking waterfront views, rich history and lively neighbourhood atmosphere.

Bay Ridge has something for everyone, from shops, boutiques, trendy clubs and cafes to cosy Italian-style hotels. Locals and visitors enjoy exploring its amenities and participating in lively local events.

Is Bay Ridge safe? Overall, Bay Ridge is a safe neighbourhood with a reported total annual crime of 2,272 (estimate) per 100,000 people.

9. Canarsie

Canarsie stands out because of its natural beauty. It is located along Jamaica Bay's coast, offering beautiful waterfront views and opportunities for outdoor activities.

It’s a middle-class neighbourhood but not ideal for those who want a more suburban feel; however, it's still pretty peaceful. The neighbourhood has a reported total annual crime of 2,510 (estimate) per 100,000 people.

10. Carroll Gardens

Carroll Garden is known for its Italian-American roots. Its heritage is celebrated in traditional Italian shops, cafes, hip boutiques, trendy restaurants, and cool bars on Court and Smith streets.

The pretty, leafy thoroughfares are lined with garden-fronted residences and brownstones, many of which are home to upwardly mobile professional and young families. Carroll Gardens has an annual crime of 2,989 (estimate) per 100,000 people.
