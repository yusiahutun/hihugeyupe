Balmain for H&amp;M Sold Out

Publish date: 2024-02-18

Update, 11/5: Balmain for H&M arrived in stores and online this morning, and fans of the brand—and the Kardashians—are going wild for it. One shopper at the Champs-Elysées store in Paris lined up at 2 PM yesterday, WWD reports, while a few minor arguments broke out at the Regent Street store in London, according to The Guardian.

Online, H&M's site crashed soon after the 8 AM launch, Footwear News reports, and the whole collection was almost sold out when the site returned around 9. Predictably, pieces have already cropped up on eBay, where some are priced at twice their retail value.

Shoppers attribute the frenzy to the popularity of the Kardashians—"I got the white blazer that Jordan Dunn wore, the basic T-shirt and another black blazer that Kim Kardashian was wearing. Seeing them in the clothes definitely influenced my decision to come here," one shopper told WWD—while other customers point to the appeal of Balmain creative director (and Instagram star) Olivier Rousteing. "I'm a huge fan of the designer himself, but not the celebrities who took part in the campaign. I couldn't care less about Kendall Jenner," said another shopper.

Update, 10/29: With less than a week until Balmain x H&M officially launches in stores, the full video campaign is finally here. Featuring Kendall Jenner leading a futuristic subway dance-off and Olivier Rousteing orchestrating the trains through some fancy, tech-y touchscreens, this is the kind of subway performance we can get behind. Although Kendall seemingly danced more in the behind-the-scenes video, the model still holds her own while playing ring-leader to a pack of dancers—offering up a few dance moves (literally only three) of her own. Update, 10/19: Ahead of Balmain x H&M's launch party celebration, slated to take place tomorrow in New York, Yahoo Style went behind-the-scenes on the campaign video with Olivier Rousteing. In the short new clip, Routsteing's model muse Kendall Jenner learns to dance for the collaboration campaign video. Jenner leads a pack of models dancing in subway cars and down futuristic metro hallways, with moves inspired by Michael Jackson's "Bad" music video. 

Rousteing also spoke out about Kendall Jenner facing the campaign in an exclusive interview with Yahoo Style, comparing her to the likes of the iconic '90s supers, "She is an icon. I'm inspired by her. For me she is the new Naomi, Claudia, Cindy. You know the type of models where people want to be her. Finally we go back to top models and are making dream girls."

And when it comes to the Balmain Army, the designer revealed that he thinks Kendall Jenner is the most important face, "Kendall had a lot of fun. It was a new experience for her. We had the best time. It was new for both of us. But more importantly, the campaign has to reflect the Balmain Army and for me, she is the most important. She also has an amazing soul and is so supportive. It is important for me to have my friends around me to support every project that I do. She's just amazing and inspires an entire generation. I love that she's global, and loves music, dancing, she's the Balmain girl."

Update, 10/9: With less than a month until the highly-anticipated Balmain x H&M collection debut, 99 images of the collaboration appear to have leaked online. Instagram user Kathryn Swartz Rees stumbled upon the collection's still-life photos on H&M's site through a Google index fluke and then shared the line with her followers, reports Fashionista. Amongst the 99 images are apparel, accessories, outerwear, and the looks worn by Gigi Hadid, Jourdan Dunn and Kendall Jenner in the official ad campaign. Update, 9/25: H&M has unveiled the campaign for their highly-anticipated designer collaboration with Balmain. The ad stars Olivier Rousteing's go-to model girl gang—Gigi Hadid, Jourdan Dunn and Kendall Jenner appearing alongside male models Hao Yun Xiang and Dudley O'Shaughnessy on a subway car (because they are budget-friendly, of course).  Rousteing's casting comes as no surprise as Jenner and Dunn were the first to debut the H&M x Balmain collection at the 2015 Billboard Music Awards last May. The collaboration will hit stores on November 5. 

Update, 8/21: ELLE France has your first look at a new dress from the Balmain for H&M collection, which drops November 5.

Original Post, 5/17: 

The next big fashion collaboration is here: Olivier Rousteing announced the Balmain for H&M Collection tonight at the Billboard Music Awards. For the big announcement, Rousteing had his right-hand It models at hand, Kendall Jenner and Jourdan Dunn, who both debuted the first looks from the upcoming collection. This collaboration follows in the footsteps of Alexander Wang for H&M, which debuted last fall.

H&M then took to Twitter to confirm the news and announce the collection's launch date, November 5, 2015.

Start lining up now—this collaboration is sure to be one of H&M's biggest yet.
