Biography of Bonnie Bernstein:

Bonnie Bernstein was born on 16th August 1970 in Brooklyn, NY but was raised in Howell, New Jersey.

She attended Howell High School and later graduated from the University of Maryland with a degree in broadcast journalism. She started her career as the news and sports director at WXJN-FM radio in Lewes, Delaware.

Later, she worked for plenty of television and became the first female sports anchor at Nevada. She came into the spotlight after she joined the ESPN channel in 1995 hosting the Michael Jordon and the Chicago Bulls’ record-setting championship run.

In the year 1998, she joined CBS sports channel and worked as a sideline reporter for the NCAA Men's Basketball Championships. Later in 2009, she again entered the ESPN channel as a field reporter for Sunday NightBaseball.
