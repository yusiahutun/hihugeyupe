Gary Lineker benched by Walkers as crisp giants release loved new advert

Publish date: 2024-06-26

Gary Lineker has been benched from Walkers Crisps’ new TV ad campaign.

The England footballer turned Match Of The Day host has been the face of the popular brand for 29 years, starring in more than 150 adverts. The new promo features the company’s spud growers lovingly cultivating their crop – but no Gary.

Fans loved the dramatic ad, set to Frankie Goes To Hollywood’s 1984 hit The Power Of Love, but noted Gaz’s absence. One tweeted: “Saw a @walkers_crisps advert with no former footballer.”

But they spoke too soon, as Gary, 62, will return, the Daily Star can reveal. He signed a £1.2million three-year deal in 2020 which saw him appear in fewer adverts and adopt an ambassadorial role, which included taking part in grassroots campaigns and tackling the company's plastic consumption.

His reps said his deal is “in the process of being extended”. Parent company PepsiCo, who bought the Walkers brand in 1999, has not responded to a request for comment.

Speaking about how the partnership first came about, Lineker previously told LeicestershireLive: "It all started when I was coming back from playing in Japan in 1994.

"It was supposed to be just a one-off collaboration but it was very well received. Then they offered me a deal, a three-year deal, a five-year deal and it has rolled on from there really and here I am still."
