Biography

James Ellroy was born in California on March 4, 1948. An American crime fiction writer, he is best known for works such as The Black Dahlia (1987) and White Jazz (1992). His densely-plotted works are marked by an economy of language and an inherent pessimism. He co-wrote the screenplay for Rampart, a film starring Woody Harrelson. He briefly served in the U.S. Army and had periods of homelessness before publishing his first novel, Brown’s Requiem, in 1981. He suffered from depression in his young hood.
