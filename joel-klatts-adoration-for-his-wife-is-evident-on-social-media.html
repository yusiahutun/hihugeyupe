The FOX analyst, who considers himself fortunate to have a loving family, is equally fortunate in his professional life.

His Professional History

Joel’s sporting career began at a young age. Gary Klatt, the head coach at Pomona High School, taught him football at first.

The analyst’s contributions to his high school football team contributed to the team winning the Jefferson County league championship and earning second-team all-state accolades.

He eventually went to baseball after taking a break from football. The San Diego Padres selected him in the eleventh round of the Major League Baseball Amateur Draft in 2000.

Joel later left the club to attend the University of Colorado and began playing college baseball, but his athletic career was short-lived.

In 2006, the former athlete began his journey into broadcasting as a sports analyst. His broadcasting career soon advanced from FOX Sports Rocky Mountain to FOX studio host, where he covered college football.

He is now known as an extremely diverse sports commentator thanks to his affiliation with the country’s premier broadcasting channel, FOX.
