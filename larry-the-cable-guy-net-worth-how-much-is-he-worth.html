Quick Facts

Who is Larry The Cable Guy?

Larry The Cable Guy whose real name is Daniel Lawrence Whitney is a cultural icon in American comedy known for his blue-collar humor and Southern twang. Born on February 17, 1963, in Pawnee City Nebraska he has carved out a niche in stand-up comedy with his persona—a lovable rough-around-the-edges character with a thick Southern accent. Larry shot to fame as part of the Blue Collar Comedy Tour alongside Jeff Foxworthy and others.

His catchphrase “Git-R-Done!” is a staple of his act and has become a part of the American lexicon. Beyond stand-up, Larry has enjoyed success as a voice actor most notably as Mater in the “Cars” franchise, and has released several gold-certified comedy albums. His work extends to television where he hosted “Only in America with Larry the Cable Guy” showcasing his exploration of American life and culture.

Career

Larry The Cable Guy has had a prolific career as a stand-up comedian actor and former radio personality.
