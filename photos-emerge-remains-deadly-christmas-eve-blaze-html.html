Photos emerge of remains from deadly Christmas Eve blaze

Publish date: 2024-06-22

Heartbreaking photos have emerged from inside a Connecticut home after a deadly Christmas blaze tore through it in 2011, killing three young children and their grandparents.

Madonna Badger was staying in the recently refurbished home on 2267 Shippan Avenue in Stamford, Connecticut on Christmas Eve with her boyfriend, parents, and three girls when the fire began.

It took the lives of her parents Lomer and Pauline Badger, and her three young daughters: nine-year-old Lily and seven-year-old twins Sarah and Grace.

Flames reportedly ripped through the home in the early hours of the morning, and trapped the children in a 'fireball' that they were unable to escape.

Photos taken by the fire marshal during the investigation have emerged as a result of a lawsuit Madonna Badger has filed against the city of Stamford.

Madonna and her then-boyfriend Michael Borcina were the only two to survive the fire.

Though the two have since split, Borcina is representing Madonna in her suit against the city, which alleges that the water hydrants surrounding the house had been disconnected for four months while the family lived there.

The city also discarded the remains of the home and bulldozed it shortly after the fire.

Ilann Maazel, a lawyer representing the estate of the girls' late father, Matthew Badger, said the disposal of potential evidence of the fire was a mistake.

'It's like a plane crash. You have to sift through the evidence to help make a determination of how it happened,' Maazel told The Post.

City officials reportedly hired a company called Servpro to clear out the home. They retrieved a few personal items of the girls, such as a teddy bear, a dress, and a diary.

They didn't, however, secure any of the family's valuables, such as jewelry or silverware.

Servpro then reportedly stripped the home of its copper wiring, steel beams, and brass fittings and sold it to a recycling company - and pocketed the profit.

Madonna Badger is alleging that the city intentionally disposed of evidence in order to cover up their potential misgivings and 'poor inspections' of the home.

Authorities said embers in a bag of discarded fireplace ashes started the blaze

Madonna's father Lomer, 71, was engulfed by flames as he tried to help his grandchildren escape.

Authorities said embers in a bag of discarded fireplace ashes started the blaze. They had been discarded by Borcina because the girls were worried Santa wouldn't be able to get down the chimney.

Lomer lived his 'dream job' of entertaining children at Saks Fifth Avenue as Santa Claus on the day that he died.

Witnesses said Mrs Badger, who ran Badger and Winters fashion branding consulting firm and created the Mark Wahlberg underwear adverts for Calvin Klein, was dazed as rescue workers helped her. 

She was heard saying: 'My whole life is in there.'

Madonna's estranged husband Matthew, who was not present during the fire, died of reportedly natural causes in February. He told Katie Couric a year after the fire that he had contemplated suicide.

Badger said in a 2012 interview that the loss of his little girls had devastated him.
