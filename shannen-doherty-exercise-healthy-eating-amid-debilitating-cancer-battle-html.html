Shannen Doherty 'back at it' with exercise and healthy eating amid debilitating cancer battle

Publish date: 2024-02-05

Shannen Doherty has some help in her battle with stage IV breast cancer.

The 48-year-old actress took to social media Saturday to let her followers know things were looking upward, as she worked out with her pals, model Anne Marie Kortright, 37 and personal trainer Kira Stokes, 45.

'After a year of dealing with cancer coming back and other stresses, I’m back at it,' she said. 'Taking care of myself and embracing every day. It’s not always easy. I have days I’m depressed or just plain lazy. But I push thru with the help of friends.'

Support system: Shannen Doherty, 48, took to social media Saturday to let her followers know things were looking upward, as she worked out with her pals, model Anne Marie Kortright, 37 and personal trainer Kira Stokes, 45.

Doherty, who played Brenda Walsh on Beverly Hills, 90210, credited Kortright with motivating her and passing along key nutritional information.

Kortright 'has been relentless in getting me to hike and learning new ways to cook that feed my soul as well as my tummy but in a very healthy way,' Doherty said, adding that she brought in Stokes for a spirited workout.

'It’s been a productive great week,' said Doherty, who's undergone a mastectomy, chemotherapy and radiation in the life-threatening battle. 'I feel better. My skin is alive and so am I.' 

It's been five years since Doherty was first diagnosed with cancer in March of 2015. She was declared cancer-free in April of 2017, but said last month that she'd been diagnosed with stage IV breast cancer.

Speaking with ABC's Good Morning America, she revealed the latest news in her health battle.

'It's going to come out in a matter of days or a week that I'm stage four, so my cancer came back, and that's why I'm here,' Doherty said. 'I don't think I've processed it. It's a bitter pill to swallow in a lot of ways.'

She said that she participated in the short-lived BH902010 reboot in an effort to pay tribute to late castmate Luke Perry, and show she could work while battling the disease.

'One of the reasons, along with Luke, that I did 90210 and didn't really tell anybody because I thought, people can look at that other people with stage four can work too,' she said. 'Like, you know, our life doesn't end the minute we get that diagnosis. We still have some living to do.'
