The best super berries

Publish date: 2024-05-16

Move over gojis, there are some new berries on the block and they're not only nutritional, but incredibly tasty. Berries are bursting with flavour, nutrients and antioxidants, so they're a great way to satisfy your sweet tooth. Whether blended into a smoothie, tossed in a salad or munched as a healthy snack, be sure to try one of these delicious fruits…

Black Mulberries

These are most delicious when added to coconut yoghurt or as a topping for smoothie bowls. Their sweet yet bitter taste has made them one of our recent favourites, but their medicinal properties have given them super-fruit status for centuries. Their powerful antioxidant comes from anthocyanins that give them a distinctive deep-purple hue, while also giving us a phytonutrient boost. They are also a good source of vitamin C and help to support healthy collagen production for firm skin.

Try: Organic Traditions Dried Black Mulberries, £3.99 at Planet Organic

Acai berries

Pronounced "ah-sah-ee", these berries hail from the Amazon rainforest and are one of the highest foods on the ORAC (oxygen radical absorbance capacity) scale, which means their antioxidant potential is very high, helping to mop up free radicals. They're a wonderful source of omega 9 (oleic acid), which helps reduce the bad cholesterol in the body, but since this manifests itself in an oil, it's important to get acai in capsule form to keep it as fresh as when it was first cultivated. Their smooth and mellow taste means you can add them to pretty much anything.

Try: Organic Burst Organic Acai Berry Capsules, £18.99 

Golden berries

Sometimes known as physalis or inca berries, golden berries are carefully protected with a delicate almost lace-like basket as they grow and can be eaten fresh or dried. Typically lower in their sugar content than other fruits, these berries also contain vitamins A and C as well as championing antioxidants such as beta-carotene and health-boosting bioflavonoids. They're versatile and delicious when added to trail mixes, homemade granolas or even kale salad.

Try: Supernutrients Organic Sundried Golden Berries, £6 at Ocado

Maqui berries

Probably not one that you would find on your typical supermarket shelves, this berry has long been revered by the Mapuche people of central and southern Chile. They're a naturally high source of vitamin C and, like other purple berries, are high in anthocyanins, which provide antioxidant benefits. Get your fix by adding the powdered version to smoothies or blend them with a cup of cashew nuts for a creamy, dairy-free cream.

Try: Planet Organic Freeze Dried Maqui Berry Powder, £15.99

Camu camu berries

Grown in flooded parts of the Amazon, these berries are about the same size as lemons and packed with vitamin C, among other nutrient-boosting flavonoid antioxidants. They also contain important amino acids that support growth and recovery. Supercharge your morning smoothie by adding in a teaspoon of camu camu powder.

Try: Detox Your World Camu Camu Powder, £11.59

British berries

While it's exciting to try exotic super berries, let's not forget that some of the ones we grow natively have just as many of health benefits. Blackberries, blueberries, raspberries and strawberries, just like their South American cousins, are sources of anthocyanin antioxidants, flavonoids and also pack a punch in terms of their vitamin C content. What's more, research has shown them to have anti-inflammatory benefits. Try to buy them as locally, seasonally and organically as possible – supporting local berry farmers is good for both the community and the environment. 

Super-berry Acai Smoothie-Bowl Recipe

Ingredients

1/4 avocado

1 small banana

1/4 cup unsweetened almond milk

1/2 tablespoon coconut oil

1/4 cup blueberries (or blackberries)

4-5 capsules of acai powder (or 1 tablespoon)

Place all of the main ingredients into a high-speed blender and blitz until you have a creamy texture. Pour into a bowl and then top with whatever you can get creative with. I've included some ideas below…
