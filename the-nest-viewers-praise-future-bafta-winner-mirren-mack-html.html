The Nest viewers praise 'future BAFTA winner' Mirren Mack

Publish date: 2024-04-22

She was sharing the screen with TV greats, but it is 22-year-old Scottish actress Mirren Mack who has emerged as the breakout star of BBC series The Nest.

Stirling-based Mack has won praise from critics and viewers alike for her 'stunning' performance as Kaya, a disadvantaged teenager whose life becomes intertwined with those of affluent couple Dan (Martin Compston) and Emily (Sophie Rundle) when she agrees to carry their baby.  

The gripping five-part drama culminated in an unexpectedly uplifting finale last night and gave Mack, who has only recently graduated from drama school, an opportunity to demonstrate her emotional range.

Compston, star of Line Of Duty, tweeted shortly after the episode aired that Mack has all the talented needed to become one of the 'greats' of Scottish acting. 

Meanwhile more than one TV critic noted in their review that 'a star is born'.

The response from viewers was similarly effusive, with one posting: 'Just finished #thenest not how I expected it to end but it was bloody good! Watch out for Mirren Mack in the future I thought she was amazing as Kaya!'

Another tweeted: 'Mirren Mack was absolutely sensational. I’ll be very surprised if we don’t see her in Big Dramas & Movies galore from now on. Agencies will surely be beating down her door. Incredible young actress.'

A third wrote: 'The showcasing of a young girl, Mirren Mack. We’ll all be able to say in 20 years time, “I remember seeing her when she first started”.'

Mack has only appeared in one other TV show but it was a similar hit: Netflix series Sex Education, in which she plays asexual Scottish schoolgirl Florence. 

The series was filmed while Mack was still a student at Guildhall School School of Music and Drama, in London, and The Nest marks her first TV role since graduating.

'For my first job after I graduated I can’t imagine anything better, I was filming back where I’m from, love and know the streets, was getting to see my family and also speaking in my own mother tongue,' ' she said in an interview with The Scotsman, referring to The Nest's Scottish location. 'It was such a gift.'

While it might seem an extraordinary stroke of luck to land such a plum job straight out of drama school, Mack has been training for years. 

The actress is the daughter of actor Billy Mack and actress/drama tutor Callan, who met while rehearsing for a play.

In the fifth year of high school in Stirling she was accepted on the Dance School of Scotland's Musical Theatre course at Knightwood, where she went onto win two prizes, before taking up a place at Guildhall School of Speech and Drama. She now splits her time between Stirling and London.

And while Mack is no doubt destined for great things, she admitted she currently has no projects in the pipeline. 

'I don’t know at the moment what’s ahead,' she added. 'I don’t know where I’m gonna be in six months and sometimes that’s quite scary and unnerving, but I like not knowing what’s coming next. I think it’s a really exciting way to live.'
