Tom Hiddleston - Bio, Age, Career, Net Worth, Height, Facts

Publish date: 2024-03-16

Table of Biography

Tom Hiddleston is a famous actor from England. Tom Hiddleston rose to international prominence as Loki in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU), first as Thor in 2011 and most recently as Loki in the Disney+ series Loki in 2021.

Early Life and Childhood

Tom Hiddleston was born in London, England on 9 February 1981. His birth name is Thomas William Hiddleston. He is 43 years old with the zodiac sign Aquarius. Tom holds British nationality and follows the Christian religion.

Caption: Tom Hiddleston with his family source: Eonline.

Regarding his family background, he is the daughter of James Norman Hiddleston (Father) and Diana Patricia Hiddleston (Mother). His mother was an arts administrator, and his father was a physical chemist. Sadly, his parents split up when he was just 12 years old. He was raised along with his two sisters, Emma and Sarah.

Tom Hiddleston – Education

Speaking of his educational background, Tom attended “Eton College” before transferring to “Pembroke College.” He then decided to study acting at the ‘Royal Academy of Dramatic Art’ (RADA). He received his diploma from ‘RADA’ in 2005.

Career and Professional Life

Talking about his career, Tom began acting while he was still in school. In 2001, he made an appearance in Stephen Whittaker’s television adaptation of “Nicholas Nickleby.” He also made an appearance in “Conspiracy” that same year. He portrayed Winston Churchill’s son in the television movie “The Gathering Storm” the following year.

Further, He made his acting debut in the 2006 British drama “Unrelated.”His sister Emma Hiddleston co-starred in the film, as did Mary Roscoe, Kathryn Worth, and David Rintoul.

Hiddleston went on to play pivotal roles in several plays over the ensuing years, including “The Challenging” (2006), “Cymbeline” (2007), and “Othello” (2008). Critics praised his performance in “Cymbeline,” which led to him winning the “Laurence Olivier Award” for “Best Newcomer in a Play” for his outstanding work.

He made an appearance in the 2011 superhero movie Thor, which was based on the same-named “Marvel Comics” character. Hiddleston played the supervillain “Loki” in the Kenneth Branagh-helmed movie. Hiddleston rose to fame internationally as a result of the movie’s commercial success.

He also starred in the Woody Allen fantasy comedy “Midnight in Paris” in the same year, playing a supporting role. The movie was a box office hit and received numerous honors, including an “Oscar.”

Caption: Tom as Loki in the Marvel Movie source: GIANT FREAKIN ROBOT.

Further, He made an appearance in “The Avengers” in 2012, a superhero movie based on the same-named superhero team from “Marvel Comics.” Hiddleston reprised his role as the supervillain “Loki” in the Joss Whedon-helmed movie.

The film depicts Loki, Thor’s brother, and fiercest foe, as he works toward achieving his goal of destroying the entire world. The movie had a huge commercial impact and made close to $1.5 billion worldwide.

More about his career

In the 2013 film “Thor: The Dark World,” Hiddleston played Loki once more. The movie, which Alan Taylor directed, was a financial success. He also portrayed the title character in Shakespeare’s original play “Coriolanus” that same year.

Since 2017, Tom has reprised his role as Loki in several Marvel films, including ‘Thor: Ragnarok’ (2017), ‘Avengers: Infinity War’ (2018), and ‘Avengers: Endgame’ (2019). All of the films went on to become massive successes that broke and set records. In the 2018 animated film “Early Man,” he also provided the voice of the character “Lord North.”

Tom made his Broadway debut in 2019 with ‘Betrayal,’ for which he received a Tony Award nomination. For his work in “The Night Manager,” he was nominated for two Primetime Emmy Awards and received his first “Golden Globe Award.”

Awards and Achievements

Tom Hiddleston has garnered several noteworthy honors throughout his career. Some of them include the “Laurence Olivier Award” for “Best Newcomer in a Play” for his performance in “Cymbeline” in 2008, the “Empire Award” for “Best Male Newcomer” for his performance in the movie “Thor” in 2012, the “MTV Movie Award” for “Best Villain” for his performance in the movie “The Avengers” in 2013. Moreover, In 2014, he earned the “Evening Standard Theatre Award” for “Best Actor” for his performance in “Coriolanus”.

In 2016, he was also honored with a “Golden Globe” in the category of “Best Actor” for the television program “The Night Manager.” For the same, he was nominated for a “Primetime Emmy.”
