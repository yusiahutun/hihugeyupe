What is Velocity Black? VIP concierge app Velocity Black

Publish date: 2024-09-07

Getty

Last week, Gigi Hadid announced that she was one of the founding members of the concierge app, Velocity Black. Alongside Poppy Delevingne, Elsa Hosk and Martha Hunt, the supermodel is backing this new idea, which claims to have reimagined the members club and concierge for the digital age.

The premise is fairly simple – download the app and "power the ultimate lifestyle from your pocket" by booking experiences. This includes everything from helicopter rides to exclusive restaurants and VIP show tickets in 60 countries around the world.

The app features a 24/seven chat feature with a guaranteed response time of one minute. For example, if you want to visit a romantic restaurant in Paris for two but have no idea where to go, the app will immediately recommend somewhere exclusive that fits your description and book you a table.

But it's not just restaurants, the app can get you access to member-only events (where you will no doubt rub shoulders with A listers), give you free upgrades at hotels, book you onto private planes, secure you on the list for private yacht parties, provide you with tickets to sold-out shows and nab you the best rooms in fancy hotels. It's basically your very own personal assistant with the kind of access only celebrities are granted.

[instagram ]https://www.instagram.com/p/BUPPFO9BhbX/?taken-by=velocity.black&hl=en[/instagram]

If all this wasn't enough, the app also allows you to attend "signature experiences" that are fairly out-of-this-world. Think a submarine ride to view the Titanic or flying to the edge of space in a fighter jet.

As some of the first members to use the app, a number of supermodels and A listers have been proving why it is worth a download. Hosk used Velocity Black to book a helicopter ride to Palm Springs, before getting a reservation at an exclusive restaurant in the area; Hunt booked an experience swimming with Orca whales in Norway; while Vanessa Hudgens hired a classic car to take her to Coachella.

Sound too good to be true? The app doesn't come cheap – annual membership to Velocity Black costs £2,000, while a one-time sign-up fee will set you back £400.
