Vern Glenn KPIX

Currently, he is working as a sports anchor at KPIX. He joined KPIX 5 and KBCWTV 44 Cable 12 in June of 2012 and first worked as a weekend Sports Anchor and weekday sports reporter and MMJ. Before KPIX 5, he worked at KRON 4 News for over two decades. Moreover, he was inducted into the SF and NorCal Silver Circle Hall of Fame in October 2020.

During his tenure at KRON 4 News, he attained his nickname “Mr. Involvement” for literally throwing himself in the middle of his feature stories. Some of his memorable stories covered are bungee jumping, skydiving, bull riding, boxing, pro football, and a dirt bike crash. He also had personal assignments like when he was reporting from the 2000 Summer Olympic Games in Sydney, Australia.

His career kicked off as a sports anchor, sports reporter, and photographer for WXEX-TV, now WRIC from 1985 to 1987. After WXEX-TV, he served as a weekend sports anchor and sports reporter at WBAL-TV in Baltimore from 1987 to 1990. In addition, Vern Joined KRON 4 News in 1990 and was part of the KNBR Radio Morning show in 1998.
