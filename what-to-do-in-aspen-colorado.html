A Weekend in Aspen, CO

Publish date: 2024-07-22

courtesy

Far-flung travel is fabulous if you’ve got the time (and budget!) to make it happen. But if you’re looking for a quick and easy getaway, allow us to remind you of the beauty of the weekend road trip. In this recurring series, we ask seasoned travelers to share all the details of their best recent trip, from the car they drove to the clothes they packed—and, of course, the must-visit locations on their itinerary.

The Destination

The past four months in New York have been anything but a winter wonderland. With spring around the corner, we've had maybe three sprinklings of snow after breaking a 50-year record for the latest measurable snowfall of the season. So when Audi invited me to Aspen for a media showcase for their debut of the 2023 Audi activesphere concept vehicle, I jumped at the chance to turn a work trip into a long weekend. I wrangled a friend from Los Angeles (without much arm-twisting, I might add) to meet me there for three days and four nights of a little work, a lot of play, and—finally—a refreshing Alpine dose of white winter.

What We Drove

Audi

The 2023 Audi SQ8 Prestige.

Aspen is a small luxury resort destination—imagine a cozy town from a Hallmark Christmas movie, but every character is on the Forbes 400—so it's very walkable, weather permitting. But if you're driving in from neighboring cities or states, or have plans to explore the slopes and sights in the surrounding area, you're going to need a car that can haul your equipment.

Audi

Inside the Audi 2023 SQ8.

We rode in a 2023 Audi SQ8 (anything else on a trip to see an Audi concept car unveiling would be like going to a concert wearing another band's tee), which was the perfect mix of practical and pampered: sliding rear seats that allow for bags, ski equipment, and, ahem, shopping hauls; heated seats; and a panoramic sunroof to gaze up at the white peaks and starry skies.

Where We Stayed

Jeremy Swanson

The Little Nell.

If you follow any celebrities or influencers who love to hit the slopes on social media, you're already familiar with The Little Nell. This boutique luxury hotel sits at the base of Aspen Mountain and is steps away from the Silver Queen Gondolas, boasting a ski-in/ski-out experience that avid skiers will want to take advantage of.

Dallas & Harris Photography

The wine room at The Little Nell.

Danielle Hamilton

The view from inside Ajax Tavern.

For those of us more interested in the "après" portion of après-ski, the resort hosts a wine bar (with a world-class wine cellar and fleet of sommeliers on staff), a speakeasy, and two on-site restaurants: Ajax Tavern, which spills out onto the mountain base and gears up into a party in the afternoon, and Element 47, a fine-dining experience for the evenings. (Tip: try the wagyu.)

Danielle Hamilton

A mountainside guest room at The Little Nell.

The rooms are spacious and inviting, but when we were able to hoist ourselves out of our fireplace-warmed beds, we found that the location was centrally situated for easy access to all the town's best offerings. Since the Little Nell is an Audi partner, they provided SUVs for exploring the surrounding mountains. But when in town, the hotel was a short walk away from local t-shirt shops; designer stores like Prada, Dior, and Louis Vuitton; the Aspen Art Museum; and myriad dining options.

The Itinerary

Thursday

Stop One: Kemo Sabe

courtesy

Inside Kemo Sabe.

My friend, a scholar of the Real Housewives franchises one and all, could not be more excited to visit the high-end Western wear boutique that featured prominently in the latest season of Beverly Hills. It's a favorite haunt of Kyle Richards, a hat obsessive, and known for its customizable cowboy hats, cowboy boots, leather goods, and turquoise jewelry. It also has a bar area (all the better for working up the nerve to drop $800 on a hat) next to which you'll find a life-sized cutout of Kathy Hilton. (She herself sent it to the store after the season aired.) It's the kind of place that really has fun with the "experience" part of its shopping experience, and we were not disappointed.

Stop Two: Campo de Fiori

Since we'd embarked on an impromptu Real Housewives of Beverly Hills tour of Aspen, it only made sense to go to the Italian restaurant where the ladies themselves dined. The Italian restaurant is big and boisterous, perfect for large parties who want to explore the ample wine list and carbo-load for a ski weekend.

Friday

Stop One: The Spa at the St. Regis Aspen Resort

courtesy

The Spa at the St. Regis.

courtesy

The Oxygen Lounge.

While my friend strapped on his skis and took advantage of the great outdoors, I decided to explore the great indoors at the nearby St. Regis. The hotel greets you with a wide-open, wood paneled lobby, and its grand grounds give you the feeling of being in a secluded chalet. But the big attraction is down the stairs at The Spa.

The spa facilities have the same warm, wood-ensconced vibes and a variety of facials, massages, and body treatments to choose from. There are also hot tubs, cold plunges, saunas, and a waterfall pool. And for newbs (like me) to Aspen's high-elevation, there's an "oxygen lounge" that will help any lingering elevation sickness you may be feeling. It's a big boost, especially on a weekend trip that doesn't allow you as much time to adjust.

Stop Two: Debuting the Audi activesphere concept vehicle

Jon Kopaloff / Getty Images for Audi

Inside the Audi activesphere concept vehicle.

It was time to get down to business at the Aspen Street Lodge, which hosts residential-style rentals and event spaces. There, Audi made the U.S. debut of their 2023 activesphere concept car.

Jon Kopaloff / Getty Images for Audi

Marc Lichte, Head of Audi Design, showing Regé-Jean Page the Audi activesphere concept vehicle.

The car is the fourth in a series of "sphere concept vehicles" designed to reflect how Audi envisions the future of driving. (The "activesphere" follows the Audi "skysphere" roadster, "grandsphere" sedan, and "urbansphere" space concept—each designed for different terrains and future-oriented technologies in mind.) It was conceived and designed at the Audi Design Studio in Malibu and partly inspired by the coastal roads of the nearby Pacific Coast Highway. It was presented in Aspen to reflect the way it imagines the demands of drivers decades from now: part Audi Sportback, part SUV, and part offroad vehicle; an electric car with autonomous chauffeuring capabilities and an augmented reality interface for navigation. The dashboard, steering wheel, and pedals disappear below the windshield when the car is in self-driving mode, opening up the already spacious cabin even more and adding to the futuristic vibe we usually only find in sci-fi movies.

Stop Three: Clark's

courtesy

Clark’s Aspen.

In the end, work trips are all about the work friends you make along the way. And several people we met at the activesphere unveiling were jonesing for a cocktail at Clark's, a local oyster bar with half-off martinis for happy hour. And if you want to go full-Aspen—where being situated 8,000 feet above sea level in landlocked Colorado won't stop you from having all the trappings of fine dining at your disposal—you can peruse its extensive raw oyster and caviar menus.

Saturday

Stop One: Aspen Art Museum

Michael Moran / OTTO

The Aspen Art Museum.

Be sure to stop by the Aspen Art Museum, designed by award-winning architect Shigeru Ban. The striking facade will immediately catch your eye and draw you in. Inside, you'll find exhibits of contemporary art and a rooftop café that's perfect for a lunchtime pit stop for food, drinks, and epic views.

Stop Two: Casa Tua

courtesy

The Library at Casa Tua.

The Aspen outpost of Casa Tua (which has locations in Miami and Paris) was the setting for a dinner celebrating the Audi activesphere, with guests Regé-Jean Page and Zoey Deutch. It's a great place for frequent visitors (the second floor houses a private club), events, or just dinner while you're in town. The vibe is "Dolomites-meets-Alpine" to compliment the Italian menu, and in-house curators furnish the space with an ever-changing rotation of new art.

Sunday

Stop One: Shopping in Downtown Aspen

courtesy

Inside Prada’s Aspen store.

Sundays are for shopping, and the first stop was the Prada store. (Marc Lichte, Head of Audi Design, had recommended we stop in to admire the simple but impeccable chalet architecture designed by Prada's go-to architect Roberto Baciocchi.) We stopped for coffee and lunch on the go at Paradise Bakery, then we hit up local boutiques like Pitkin County Dry Goods, where we lucked out on a 70%-off sale that tested the tensile strength of our luggage on the way home.

Stop Two: The Living Room at Hotel Jerome

courtesy

The Living Room at Hotel Jerome.

We winded down the weekend with cocktails at the bar in the lobby of Hotel Jerome. It's a lively and tightly-packed space on a Friday or Saturday night, but on Sundays its sumptuous but homey set-up makes for a more quiet evening—perfect for one last nightcap.
